Signal Confirmation

Indicators

Indicators are tools that help you read momentum, direction, and volatility. They do not predict the future. They help you measure what price is already doing.

MACD

MACD compares short-term and long-term momentum. Traders often use it to spot momentum shifts and possible continuation or reversal setups.

Moving Averages

Moving averages smooth out price so trend direction is easier to see. If price stays above a moving average, trend direction may be bullish. If it stays below, trend direction may be bearish.

RSI

RSI measures momentum strength on a scale. It can help show when momentum is fading or when a move is extended.

Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands measure price expansion and contraction. When price pushes outside the bands, it can signal volatility, extension, or the need for confirmation from another tool.

Indicator Confirmation

Indicators work better when they agree with what price action is showing. A pattern plus trend direction plus indicator confirmation is usually stronger than one random signal by itself.

Keep It Simple

Best use of indicators

  • Use them to confirm price action
  • Do not stack random tools together
  • Wait for location plus momentum to match
