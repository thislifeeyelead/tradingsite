What Is Trading?

Trading is the act of buying or selling a financial asset based on price movement. For beginners, the first goal is not trying to get rich in twelve minutes. It is learning how the market moves and what the chart is actually showing.

Market Basics

Markets move because buyers and sellers are constantly reacting to price, news, momentum, and emotion. A chart is just a visual record of that fight.

How to Read the Chart

Start with the overall direction. Is price moving up, down, or sideways? Then look at where price keeps reacting. That helps you spot support, resistance, and momentum shifts.

Timeframes

Shorter timeframes move faster and create more noise. Higher timeframes usually show the bigger trend more clearly.

Candlesticks

Candlesticks show open, high, low, and close. The body shows whether price finished higher or lower than it started, while the wicks show how far price moved during that time.

Support and Resistance

Support is an area where price often stops falling and reacts upward. Resistance is an area where price often stops rising and reacts downward.

Risk and Reality

No setup wins every time. Even strong-looking trades fail. Risk management matters because one bad decision can undo a lot of decent ones.
